# **Nansana**

**Reducing youth unemployment by 25% and creating 30,000 quality jobs for young people across four districts.**

Nansana Municipality is leveraging data-driven approaches to tackle youth unemployment and empower young people across its four districts. The AMALI Data Programme has supported Mayor Regina Bakitte Musoke and her city team to develop performance management systems, structure existing data, and design monitoring tools that track progress toward creating decent employment opportunities. Through sustained coaching and technical assistance, the city is building the institutional foundations needed to move from fragmented interventions to a coordinated, evidence-based strategy for youth economic inclusion. 

## **Highlights**

| Established a structured mayoral legacy goal to reduce youth unemployment by 25% (122,000 young people) and create 30,000 quality and decent jobs by 2026\. |

| Conducted a comprehensive data inventory across multiple departments, enabling the city team to identify data gaps and align existing interventions to the legacy goal. |
| Conceptualised a data reporting tool to support performance management, monitoring, and evaluation of youth-focused city activities and government programmes. |

## **Background**

Nansana Municipality, located in close proximity to the capital Kampala, is one of Uganda’s most densely populated urban areas and an attractive destination for people seeking economic opportunities. The Municipality’s four districts — Busukuma, Gombe, Nabweru, and Nansana — face significant challenges including high poverty, unemployment, unplanned development, and limited infrastructure. These conditions have contributed to rising youth crime, teenage pregnancy, drug use, and involvement in unsavoury activities, largely driven by a lack of economic opportunity for young people.

Mayor Regina Bakitte Musoke, a civil servant with over 12 years of government experience across multiple ministries, has placed youth empowerment at the centre of her agenda. As chairperson of the Alliance of Mayors and Municipal Leaders’ Initiative for Community Action on AIDS at the Local Level (Uganda chapter), the Mayor brings both institutional experience and a deeply personal commitment to supporting young people. The AMALI Data Programme engagement has supported the Municipality to develop the data practices, team alignment, and performance monitoring systems necessary to translate this political commitment into measurable progress on youth unemployment.

## **Impact**

Through the AMALI Data Programme, Nansana Municipality has fundamentally restructured how it organises and utilises data related to youth employment. The city team, comprising approximately 12 officials spanning planning, IT, community development, labour, and youth coordination, has learned to better align existing government programmes to the Mayor’s legacy goal. Where previously multiple interventions operated in isolation with no clear connection to unemployment reduction, the team can now articulate how each initiative contributes to the broader strategy. The data inventory process revealed critical gaps in how beneficiary information was captured and tracked, prompting the development of a new data reporting tool designed to provide accurate, real-time insights into programme performance. Beyond internal systems, the AMALI engagement has catalysed a shift in how the Municipality engages with young people and the broader community. 

The Mayor’s visibility through recreational activities has already contributed to a reported reduction in crime, with feedback from local courts and police confirming this trend. The city team now recognises young people as primary stakeholders who must be actively involved in programme design and implementation. Improved coordination between Community Development Officers, the Planning Unit, and the Labour office has created new pathways for reaching youth across all four districts. 

**Significance Factor for this work in Masaka City**

Nansana’s journey illustrates the complexity and importance of tackling youth unemployment in a rapidly growing African municipality operating under significant resource constraints. Youth employment is a global concern, and the decision by a peri-urban municipality adjacent to a capital city to anchor its response in data-driven governance is significant. It demonstrates that evidence-based approaches to one of Africa’s most pressing demographic challenges can be led by local government leaders who are willing to invest in building institutional capacity, even where existing systems are yet to fully be developed and data practices are still maturing.

This work also positions Nansana as an emerging model for how municipalities can address youth disengagement holistically, combining economic empowerment with recreational programming, community-building, and behavioural change initiatives. By acknowledging that not all interventions need to generate direct monetary outcomes to be impactful, the Municipality is pioneering an approach that values cultural and social shifts alongside employment creation. As Nansana matures its data systems, strengthens cross-departmental collaboration, and formalises its legacy goal within budgetary and institutional processes, it creates a replicable framework for peer cities navigating similar youth employment challenges across the continent.
